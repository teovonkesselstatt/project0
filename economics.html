One of the crucial decisions many teenagers have to make is what to study and where. I was always sure I would study Engeneering, as it was what everybody told me, as well as what I told myself. But in my last years of high school, I found myself immersed in Economics, a science that proved to be the perfect combination of Mathematics and Social Sciences.

I have been studying Economics at Universidad Torcuato di Tella for the last two years and I cannot imagine myself anywhere else. I was fortunate enough to decide what was right for me more than two years ago and my interest does not stop growing.

I find increadibly amusing 3 specific aspects of economics:

How every voluntary transaction in the market is mutually beneficial, as long as there is no asymetric information.
How billions of people agree on the exchange of goods with no intrinsec value, such as money, for goods totally necessary for substenance, as food.
How phenomena as inflation and economic growth can occurr with or without government intervention, as well as the literature that supports each ideology.

I thank two-years-ago Theo for doing what was the optimal decision for him, as well as for me.
